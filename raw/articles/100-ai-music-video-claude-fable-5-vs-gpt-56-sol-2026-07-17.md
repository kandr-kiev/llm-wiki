6v6l4 2"></path></svg> <!-- -->8<!-- --> min read</span></div></header><div class="prose-tryai"><p>We built a small agentic harness with one job: hand a model a song, a hard dollar budget, and a set of tools, then get out of the way and let it produce a full music video on its own. The model researches which video models exist, generates clips, watches its own footage, edits with ffmpeg, and assembles a final cut.</p>
<p>A few readers of our <a node="[object Object]" href="/blog/gpt-5.6-build-off-12-models">last build-off</a> said they wanted to see how tool use actually varies between models, so we gave frontier-level models an open-ended, long-horizon task where each model decides on its own what to research, what to generate, and how to edit. We log every tool call, so you can see exactly how each one worked (full transcripts below).</p>
<p>We ran two models, <a node="[object Object]" href="/models/claude-fable-5">Claude Fable 5</a> and <a node="[object Object]" href="/models/gpt-5.6-sol">GPT-5.6 Sol</a>, each at two budgets ($25 and $100), for four runs total. Every run got the same song (Bruno Mars and Mark Ronson&#x27;s &quot;Uptown Funk&quot;), a short text description, and a time-stamped lyric transcript.</p>
<h2 id="the-setup">The setup</h2>
<p>Each model ran an autonomous tool-calling loop with six tools:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>plan</strong>: a tool for thinking (no cost, no action).</li>
<li><strong>web_search</strong>: to research generation models and their APIs and fetch information about music videos (if needed).</li>
<li><strong>get_budget</strong>: to check the remaining budget.</li>
<li><strong>generate_image</strong> and <strong>generate_video</strong>: the only tools that spend budget. The model can pick any FAL or Replicate model and pass its own parameters.</li>
<li><strong>run_command</strong>: a local shell with ffmpeg/ffprobe available, used to analyze audio, cut and concatenate clips, and mux the final video.</li>
</ul>
<p>Once the budget hits zero, paid generation is refused, but the model can keep editing. Every model message, tool call, charge, and error was logged. The whole harness is open source at <a href="https://github.com/hershalb/music-video-arena" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer" node="[object Object]">github.com/hershalb/music-video-arena</a>, so you can run it yourself.</p>

<h2 id="our-take">Our take</h2>
<p>None of the music videos were great, but watching how the models got there was pretty interesting and does show where gaps still clearly exist for frontier-level models. A few things notes:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Character and story consistency was a struggle for all four.</strong> Recurring characters drift between shots, and none of the videos hold a coherent storyline from start to finish.</li>
<li><strong>The models take lyrics very literally.</strong> &quot;Make a dragon wanna retire, man&quot; gets you an actual dragon on screen. It&#x27;s interesting for a few shots, but got a little weird after a while.</li>
<li><strong>Tempo matching is weak.</strong> The cuts land on the beat (they all ran the ffmpeg beat detection), but the motion inside the clips, dancing, camera moves, rarely matches the song&#x27;s tempo, so it often feels a little off. An example line &quot;gotta kiss myself I&#x27;m so pretty&quot;, shows the main character making a kissing motion way too slowly.</li>
<li><strong>GPT-5.6 Sol at $25 was the most inventive editor.</strong> It overlaid text and animated still images with video effects, techniques none of the other runs tried. The rest mostly just stitched generated clips together. GPT 5.6 Sol $100 also tried multiple video models instead of just sticking with one like Fable did.</li>
<li><strong>Nobody really iterated on the edit.</strong> Once clips existed, the models concatenated and muxed, but rarely went back to re-cut or add effects, and none seriously probed their own clips to confirm they were any good. GPT-5.6 Sol&#x27;s $100 run shipped some genuinely low-quality AI clips, while Claude Fable 5 happened to pick a model with more coherent output. Some of this is probably a model limitation, but the lack of self-review is notable.</li>
<li><strong>Neither model touched Replicate.</strong> Both FAL and Replicate keys were available, but all four runs used FAL exclusively.</li>
<li><strong>Claude Fable 5 was the pricier pick.</strong> It cost more per run (and the most overall, at $73.65) despite finishing faster than GPT-5.6 Sol. Subjectively, we slightly preferred the Fable $100 video, though none blew us away.</li>
<li><strong>$100 was probably too much budget.</strong> Neither model wanted to spend near the cap, and both kept their step counts modest. With that headroom they could have, for example, generated consistent character images up front and animated from those, but neither chose to.</li>
</ul>
<p>We&#x27;ll see if models can improve on more subjective/stylistic tasks as they continue to get smarter, but for now there&#x27;s still a lot of room for improvement.</p></div><div
