Social media is one of the hardest domains to study here — much harder than, say, <a target="_self" class="group inline text-pangram-orange hover:underline underline" href="https://www.pangram.com/ai-news">news articles</a>, <a target="_self" class="group inline text-pangram-orange hover:underline underline" href="https://www.pangram.com/blog/pangram-predicts-21-of-iclr-reviews-are-ai-generated">research papers</a>, or <a target="_self" class="group inline text-pangram-orange hover:underline underline" href="https://www.pangram.com/blog/ai-amazon-reviews">Amazon reviews</a>. But it&#x27;s also one of the most crucial, because it&#x27;s potentially the highest-volume source of AI-generated content we face.</p>
<p class="mb-4 font-heading text-pangram-black leading-8 font-normal text-lg">We believe it&#x27;s important to understand this problem so that we can better combat it. That&#x27;s why we included an opt-in setting in our Chrome extension, to allow users to aid our research by anonymously sharing their scan statistics with us. We&#x27;ve compiled the first few months of this data into the report below.</p>
<h4 id="key-findings" class="mb-8 heading2 ppeiko mt-8 scroll-mt-40 text-xl font-heading font-light leading-tight text-pangram-text-primary md:scroll-mt-24">Key findings:</h4>
<ul class="mb-4 ml-3 list-[square] pl-3 marker:text-pangram-orange">
<li class="my-1 text-pangram-black font-body leading-6 font-normal text-base">One in four longform posts on social media flagged as fully AI-generated</li>
<li class="my-1 text-pangram-black font-body leading-6 font-normal text-base">Of all posts we flagged as AI-generated, two-thirds came from LinkedIn</li>
<li class="my-1 text-pangram-black font-body leading-6 font-normal text-base">Nearly half of X/Twitter articles contained AI writing</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="ai-generated-content-is-hitting-longform-the-hardest" class="ppeiko mb-4 mt-8 scroll-mt-40 text-3xl font-heading font-light leading-tight text-pangram-text-primary md:scroll-mt-24">AI-generated content is hitting longform the hardest</h2>
<p class="mb-4 font-heading text-pangram-black leading-8 font-normal text-lg">AI-generated content appeared across all social media platforms in our data set. The average AI rate across all scanned items was 13.8%, but specific rates varied by platform and item length. On four out of five platforms, <strong>longer content was more likely to be AI-generated than shortform content</strong>. Across all platforms, one in four longform items (25.72% of items over 250 words) were fully AI-generated.</p>
<p class="mb-4 font-heading text-pangram-black leading-8 font-normal text-lg"><span class="my-6 block"><span class="flex flex-col items-center"><img alt="" loading="lazy" width="1200" height="1200" decoding="async" data-nimg="1" class="h-auto max-h-[600px] max-w-full object-contain" style="color:transparent" srcSet="/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fcdn.sanity.io%2Fimages%2F6vunltag%2Fproduction%2F7b75f2c3b79d08f87fe4ca67de8a8fa7433e0383-2400x1260.png&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75 1x, /_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fcdn.sanity.io%2Fimages%2F6vunltag%2Fproduction%2F7b75f2c3b79d08f87fe4ca67de8a8fa7433e0383-2400x1260.png&amp;w=3840&amp;q=75 2x" src="/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fcdn.sanity.io%2Fimages%2F6vunltag%2Fproduction%2F7b75f2c3b79d08f87fe4ca67de8a8fa7433e0383-2400x1260.png&amp;w=3840&amp;q=75"></span></span></p>
<p class="mb-4 font-heading text-pangram-black leading-8 font-normal text-lg">Substack was an exception; there, the rate of fully AI-generated content remained fairly flat, and longer, more substantial posts were actually slightly less likely to be AI-generated compared to shorter ones.</p>
<p class="mb-4 font-heading text-pangram-black leading-8 font-normal text-lg">LinkedIn was the most AI-saturated platform, where <strong>more than 40% of longform posts</strong> flagged as fully AI-generated. However, if we included mixed AI and human content, X/Twitter was the worst off: almost half of X articles were either fully AI-generated (23.9%) or <a target="_self" class="group inline text-pangram-orange hover:underline underline" href="https://www.pangram.com/blog/introducing-ai-assistance-detection">AI-assisted</a>/mixed (22.9%), with <strong>only 53.2% of X articles flagging as fully human-authored</strong>.</p>
<p class="mb-4 font-heading text-pangram-black leading-8 font-normal text-lg"><span class="my-6 block"><span class="flex flex-col items-center"><img alt="" loading="lazy" width="1200" height="1200" decoding="async" data-nimg="1" class="h-auto max-h-[600px] max-w-full object-contain" style="color:transparent" srcSet="/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fcdn.sanity.io%2Fimages%2F6vunltag%2Fproduction%2Fa46788d43a127da59b1596c750b1ce544c0122d0-1988x1040.png&amp;w=1200&amp;q=75 1x, /_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fcdn.sanity.io%2Fimages%2F6vunltag%2Fproduction%2Fa46788d43a127da59b1596c750b1ce544c0122d0-1988x1040.png&amp;w=3840&amp;q=75 2x" src="/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fcdn.sanity.io%2Fimages%2F6vunltag%2Fproduction%2Fa46788d43a127da59b1596c750b1ce544c0122d0-1988x1040.png&amp;w=3840&amp;q=75"></span></span></p>
<p class="mb-4 font-heading text-pangram-black leading-8 font-normal text-lg">Our data shows that AI-generated content is a problem across all platforms, and it is hitting longform content especially hard. Even Substack, which was the longform platform with the lowest combined AI rate, still saw more than a fifth of its posts (21.9%) flag as AI-generated or AI-assisted. This is consistent with the rise of AI-generated content we&#x27;re seeing in writing elsewhere, such as in <a rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" class="group inline text-pangram-orange hover:underline underline" href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.18774">newspaper opinion pieces</a>.</p>
<h2 id="ai-slop-is-flooding-linkedin" class="ppeiko mb-4 mt-8 scroll-mt-40 text-3xl font-heading font-light leading-tight text-pangram-text-primary md:scroll-mt-24">AI slop is flooding LinkedIn</h2>
<p class="mb-4 font-heading text-pangram-black leading-8 font-normal text-lg">LinkedIn had the highest AI share of any platform across the board. LinkedIn posts made up a third of scanned items, yet it accounted for <strong>nearly two-thirds (62%) of all AI content we flagged</strong>. Contrary to what one might expect, people are overwhelmingly willing to use AI to speak on their behalf in professional settings that are associated with their real identity, and less likely to use it on casual and anonymous platforms.</p>
