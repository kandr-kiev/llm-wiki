        <h1>businesses with ugly AI menu redesigns!!!</h1>

        <p>
            <i>
                <time datetime="2026-07-20T16:37Z">
    20 Jul, 2026
</time>
            </i>
        </p>
    

    <p>I stopped by a Filipino/Hawaiian (mostly filipino) restaurant/bar that was recommended to me a few months ago by someone I met at an AANHPI event. One of my friends that was eating with us had gone many times before and said they enjoyed the menu.</p>
<p>We looked up the menu before heading over.</p>
<p><img src="https://bear-images.sfo2.cdn.digitaloceanspaces.com/yap/unnamed.webp" alt="unnamed" /></p>
<p>As a Filipino-American I always get curious about Filipino restaurants because a lot of our dishes are family-style/comfort food, so it’s always interesting to see what foods will be on the menu.</p>
<p>To our disappointment (did I mention the friend group I was eating with are my artist friends and we had just gone to a doodle date meetup?) the restaurant revamped their menu with AI. NO!</p>
<p><img src="https://bear-images.sfo2.cdn.digitaloceanspaces.com/yap/img_1149.webp" alt="IMG_1149" /></p>
<p>So ill. I hate it. Uncanny. UGH.</p>
<p>The worst part is that I frequently work with small businesses in Austin and know that this is from a place of complete ignorance rather than feeling a sense of ownership in art/design they did not do.</p>
<p>People see button. They press.</p>
<p>NO!!!</p>
<p><img src="https://bear-images.sfo2.cdn.digitaloceanspaces.com/yap/img_1150.webp" alt="IMG_1150" /></p>
<p>The disturbing uncanny-like look of all these plates are so… bad. I’m sorry to subject you, the reader, to such slop.</p>
<p>I was thinking about ordering the <em>adobo</em> plate before I got to the restaurant and then pivoted to <em>spamsilog</em> instead (lmao I was craving comfort food). The plate was only $10. I could make this at home but often don’t have the patience to get the spam really crispy (also I get to lazy so make <em>sinangan</em>/garlic rice)… and also I use the low sodium spam at home.</p>
<p>Ugly AI pic of the plate vs the real plate.</p>
<p><img src="https://bear-images.sfo2.cdn.digitaloceanspaces.com/yap/img_1152.webp" alt="IMG_1152" />
<img src="https://bear-images.sfo2.cdn.digitaloceanspaces.com/yap/img_1151.webp" alt="IMG_1151" /></p>
<p>Disturbing AI aside this tasted just like waking up at home and my mom cooking spam for breakfast.</p>
<p>This is extremely conflicting because I love to support/shop small and local businesses and I’ve seen more and more of them use genAI in their signage. Yes I also understand many of them have slim margins, but TBH I would rather read a whole menu in Comic Sans, Papyrus, or Curlz MT than this garbage.</p>

          function formatDate(dateStr, formatStr) {
            const date = new Date(dateStr);
            const day = date.getDate();
            const month = date.getMonth();
            const year = date.getFullYear();
            const weekday = date.getDay();
            const hours = date.getHours();
            const minutes = date.getMinutes();
            const monthsFull = ['January', 'February', 'March', 'April', 'May', 'June', 'July', 'August', 'September', 'October', 'November', 'December'];
            const monthsShort = ['Jan', 'Feb', 'Mar', 'Apr', 'May', 'Jun', 'Jul', 'Aug', 'Sep', 'Oct', 'Nov', 'Dec'];
            const daysFull = ['Sunday', 'Monday', 'Tuesday', 'Wednesday', 'Thursday', 'Friday', 'Saturday'];
            const daysShort = ['Sun', 'Mon', 'Tue', 'Wed', 'Thu', 'Fri', 'Sat'];
            
            function getOrdinal(n) {
                const s = ['th', 'st', 'nd', 'rd'];
                const v = n % 100;
                return s[(v - 20) % 10] || s[v] || s[0];
            }
            
            const map = {
                'd': () => day.toString().padStart(2, '0'),
                'm': () => (month + 1).toString().padStart(2, '0'),
                'Y': () => year.toString(),
                'y': () => year.toString().slice(-2),
                'F': () => monthsFull[month],
                'j': () => day.toString(),
                'D': () => daysShort[weekday],
                'l': () => daysFull[weekday],
                'S': () => getOrdinal(day),
                'M': () => monthsShort[month],
                'H': () => hours.toString().padStart(2, '0'),
                'h': () => {
                    let h = hours % 12;

                },
                'i': () => minutes.toString().padStart(2, '0'),

                'A': () => hours < 12 ? 'AM' : 'PM',
            };
            
            let result = '';
            for (let char of formatStr) {
                result += map[char] ? map[char]() : char;
            }
            return result;
