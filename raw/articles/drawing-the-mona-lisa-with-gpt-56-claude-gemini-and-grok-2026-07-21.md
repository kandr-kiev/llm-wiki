The model sets a color, tip width, and pressure, lays down batches of strokes, smudges to blend, erases, and calls view_canvas to see its own work and decide what to fix. <p>We ran four vision models, <a node="[object Object]" href="/models/gpt-5.6-sol">GPT-5.6 Sol</a>, <a node="[object Object]" href="/models/claude-fable-5">Claude Fable 5</a>, <a node="[object Object]" href="/models/grok-4.5">Grok 4.5</a>, and <a node="[object Object]" href="/models/gemini-3.6-flash">Gemini 3.6 Flash</a>, across two targets (the Mona Lisa and Van Gogh&#x27;s Starry Night, both scored objectively) and five open-ended prompts, for 28 drawings total. We&#x27;ll cover tool use, cost, output, and whether the models actually improved their work, with our opinion at the end.</p>
<h2 id="why-we-run-these">Why we run these</h2>
<p>A quick note on why we&#x27;re doing this, because our <a node="[object Object]" href="/blog/ai-music-video-arena-claude-vs-gpt-5.6">last one of these</a> (models making music videos) kicked off a good <a href="https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48939524" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer" node="[object Object]">discussion</a> where some folks read it as us promoting the creativity or artistic ability of models. These are not objective tests of model capability. They are deliberately open-ended, fuzzy tasks. Here is why we personally keep running them:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>They are fun, and genuinely informative.</strong> Watching a model tackle a loose, open-ended task is a more interesting visual indicator of capability than yet another benchmark number.</li>
<li><strong>They expose the real frontier-vs-open gap.</strong> Cheaper open-weight models can absolutely replace the frontier for a lot of execution work, and we will keep reporting on that. But there is also a lot of benchmaxxing out there, and tasks like this cut through it. They genuinely separate frontier capability from the rest. Grok 4.5, as you will see, was rough at this &quot;basic&quot; drawing task, and the open-weight models we tried were not even usable, several just returned a blank canvas. (That may change with Kimi K3 which we will report on once it is fully open-sourced)</li>
<li><strong>They show what long-running tasks actually cost.</strong> Claude Fable 5 almost always took much longer than the others, for far more money, and here produced worse output. Fable has beaten GPT-5.6 for us on plenty of tasks, including the <a node="[object Object]" href="/blog/ai-music-video-arena-claude-vs-gpt-5.6">music-video challenge</a>, but on this one it was worse for a lot more overhead. Depending on your use case, that trade-off matters.</li>
<li><strong>We&#x27;re loving the discussions.</strong> The discussions have genuinely been interesting, and if you have suggestions for the setup or new tasks, we will take them into account. These have been a blast to run, and the outputs are super exciting to see.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="the-tools">The tools</h2>
<p>Every model worked with the exact same colored-pencil toolset:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>plan</strong>: a no-op scratchpad for thinking and planning between steps.</li>
<li><strong>view_target</strong>: look at the target image again.</li>
<li><strong>view_canvas</strong>: render the current page and see it. This is also when we score the drawing against the target.</li>
<li><strong>set_color</strong> / <strong>set_brush</strong> / <strong>set_pressure</strong>: the current pencil color, tip width, and pressure (0 to 1 opacity, low pressure for softer tone).</li>
<li><strong>draw</strong>: lay down a batch of marks (strokes, lines, shape outlines, dots) in one call. There are no solid fills, tone and color are built by layering, like a real pencil.</li>
<li><strong>smudge</strong>: blend/soften a rectangular region, like a blending stump.</li>
<li><strong>erase</strong> / <strong>clear_canvas</strong>: lift a region back to white / reset the whole page.</li>
</ul>
<p>The whole harness is open source at <a href="https://github.com/hershalb/canvas-arena" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer" node="[object Object]">github.com/hershalb/canvas-arena</a>. Point it at any target image or a text prompt and run it yourself.</p>
