<h3 id="pebble-software-progress-and-roadmap" class="group"><a href="#pebble-software-progress-and-roadmap" class="anchor-link"><span class="anchor-hash">#</span>Pebble Software - Progress and Roadmap</a></h3>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">Over the last 6 months, the core four person Pebble software team built and shipped a metric ton of new Pebble open source software! Our improvements were centered around these areas:</p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800"><strong>Battery life</strong></p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">We’ve (well, mostly Gerard 🙂) worked extraordinarily hard over the last few months, optimizing and reducing power consumption in PebbleOS. As <a href="https://ericmigi.com/blog/how-to-build-a-smartwatch-software-setting-expectations-and-roadmap/#near-term-software-r" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-[#FA4A36] hover:text-[#e8432f]">predicted</a>, we boosted the median battery life of Pebble 2 Duo from 17 days (last summer) to over 30 days. Pebble Time 2 median is currently around 21 days - more improvements in the works here too! The biggest consumers of power are backlight, watchfaces with a lot of animations and health tracking. If you want to ‘hypermile’ your Pebble, try switching to a low-animation watchface and the new <em>Battery Saver</em> backlight mode (Settings → Display → Backlight).</p>

<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800"><strong>Stability</strong></p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">Thanks to <a href="https://help.repebble.com/en/articles/15403206-get-help-contact-us-bug-reports" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-[#FA4A36] hover:text-[#e8432f]">helpful bug reports </a>from y’all, we’ve made hundreds of small improvements to PebbleOS and the Pebble mobile app. Please keep it coming!</p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">I’ll dive into one specific (and ultra technical) topic - reverse PPoGATT (Pebble Protocol over GATT). Quick history: during the first Pebble era, we configured the Pebble mobile app to expose a PPoGATT service, as means to work around the lack of IPC between iOS apps. This setup is the opposite of how Bluetooth accessories normally connect to phones and caused a number of weird problems! Also this setup blocks us from using iOS AccessorySetupKit (ASK), which is a prerequisite for us to implement the new Notification Forwarding feature (EU only) that will finally enable you to reply to notifications. Enabling ASK is going to be tough - our iOS app must either use ASK or not, meaning that we need to upgrade the recovery firmware on all Pebble watches in the field to reverse PPoGATT before we can switch ASK on. Anyways, we have the first piece of the puzzle in place (Pebble Round 2’s recovery firmware already has the upgrade). This saga will take a while.</p>

<h3 id="pebble-time-2-problems-you-ve-reported" class="group"><a href="#pebble-time-2-problems-you-ve-reported" class="anchor-link"><span class="anchor-hash">#</span>Pebble Time 2 - Problems You’ve Reported</a></h3>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">Thank you all for reporting any bugs or issues you’ve spotted! We test each watch at the factory before it’s shipped out, and we test each software release internally and with a growing team of beta testers (want to join? Sign up at <a class="text-[#FA4A36] hover:text-[#e8432f]" href="/account">rePebble.com/account</a>). But these tests are not infallible and we will make mistakes. We appreciate your reports as they help us get more information to help us fix problems!</p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800"><strong>Software Issues</strong></p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">We’re tracking three big software issues with PebbleOS, and a multitude of smaller problems. While we are actively working on fixing these with a future software , we don’t have an ETA on when these will be fixed.</p>
<ul class="list-disc pl-6 mb-4 space-y-1 marker:text-gray-500">
<li class="pl-1">Step and sleep tracking metrics are not accurate for some people</li>
<li class="pl-1">Accelerometer sometimes stops working</li>
<li class="pl-1">Touch screen sometimes stops working or registers touches in wrong location</li>
</ul>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">It would be tough to list here the long-tail of software issues we’ve had reported. But please note that while we don’t reply to everyone, we do read every single report and look for patterns and clues that help us fix many issues with each software update (see the changelog for <a href="https://ndocs.repebble.com/pebbleos-changelog" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-[#FA4A36] hover:text-[#e8432f]">PebbleOS</a> and <a href="https://ndocs.repebble.com/changelog" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-[#FA4A36] hover:text-[#e8432f]">Pebble mobile app</a>).</p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800"><strong>Hardware Issues</strong></p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">You’ve all demonstrated incredible patience waiting for your PT2 to ship. You’re excited to try the first brand new Pebble in the last 10 years. That’s why we understand how painful and difficult it could be if you unbox your brand new watch and discover manufacturing flaw, or use it for a few weeks and find the battery is dying too quickly or accidentally crack the glass. It sucks!</p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">We feel your pain, even more than you can possibly imagine. That’s why everyone who has reported a hardware issue to our support team has received a free replacement (with free worldwide shipping) regardless of whether their device is under warranty or not.</p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">To date, we’ve replaced 330 PT2s (out of 17.82 million hours of usage from 19,000+ watches in the field).</p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">Mass producing a consumer electronic product is labour intensive. Making stuff is still a very human-centric process. We make mistakes. A worker may not assemble a part correctly. A test may be accidentally skipped. The test result could be read incorrectly. Procedures can be put in place to minimize mistakes, but the cost will rise. As with all of hardware product development - it’s a tradeoff 🤷.</p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">The most frequent hardware issue we’re seeing is very high power consumption (less than ~3 day battery life). We’ve taken apart some units and found a variety of issues. To combat this, we’ve implemented more stringent power consumption testing on the assembly line. If you encounter this issue (regardless of your warranty eligibility), please send us a <a href="https://help.repebble.com/en/articles/15403206-get-help-contact-us-bug-reports" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-[#FA4A36] hover:text-[#e8432f]">bug report</a> in the Pebble app and we can help you out!</p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">Next most frequent are problems with the touch panel. At first, we thought this could be a hardware problem and replaced around 70 watches. After reviewing the units with our factory, we now believe this could be a software bug. We’re working to fix these issues with a software update - if we can’t, we’ll replace the affected watches (regardless of your warranty eligibility).</p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">Next up is the front glass cracking. We’ve had 51 reports so far, and we’ve sent a free replacement to each person affected. If your glass has cracked, send us a video (preferably, picture is ok) in a <a href="https://help.repebble.com/en/articles/15403206-get-help-contact-us-bug-reports" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-[#FA4A36] hover:text-[#e8432f]">bug report</a> in the Pebble app. During the lead up to mass production, we performed extensive environmental testing - including <a href="https://www.youtube.com/shorts/An9e-X7rIkQ" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-[#FA4A36] hover:text-[#e8432f]">drop testing</a>, <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=beguiHL4mBE" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-[#FA4A36] hover:text-[#e8432f]">tumble testing</a>, button press, strap <a href="https://youtu.be/xeDa1SR2KWU" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-[#FA4A36] hover:text-[#e8432f]">stretch</a> and <a href="https://youtu.be/vtU0iPFaPdc" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-[#FA4A36] hover:text-[#e8432f]">bend</a>, thermal cycling and many other tests. All test results showed normal durability compared to similar smartwatches. But if your watch glass cracks, do you care what the factory test results were? Or that this has happened to just 0.25% of all PT2s - or once every 30+ years of usage? Of course not - your watch just broke. That’s why we will continue replacing reasonable reports of glass cracking for free as long as we can. At some point, we will shift to offering a replacement at a highly discounted amount. We are also looking into sourcing extra LCM modules (the entire front assembly - glass, touch panel, display, metal top cover and backlight) and making them available for folks who choose to fix their watch themselves.</p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">The final big category of hardware issue are reports of button problems (32 so far). In some cases, a small interior clip is improperly assembled, causing the button to pop off. We’ve addressed this issue with changes to the production line process and hope that it becomes much less frequent as watches assembled after the change start making their way out into the world. If you encounter this issue (regardless of your warranty eligibility), please send us a <a href="https://help.repebble.com/en/articles/15403206-get-help-contact-us-bug-reports" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-[#FA4A36] hover:text-[#e8432f]">bug report</a> in the Pebble app and we can help you out!</p>
<p class="mb-4 text-gray-800">Then we’ve had a long tail of smaller issues that I’m moderately embarrassed by, like a report of the watch missing screws on the bottom, or the <a href="https://imgur.com/a/6D5PLoA" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-[#FA4A36] hover:text-[#e8432f]">front falling off</a>. I guess these things <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3m5qxZm_JqM" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="text-[#FA4A36] hover:text-[#e8432f]">do happen</a>!</p>
