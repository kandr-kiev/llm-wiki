For its first quarter century or so, the traffic was manageable and mostly polite. As Bruce puts it:</p><blockquote><p>Pre-AI, we got human traffic, mostly well-behaved search engine crawlers, and a few people crawling the site for personal projects. If someone got too greedy, we could spot them and block them.</p></blockquote><p><span>Over the past couple of years, website owners the world over have seen their web traffic change. What was initially only people browsing gave way to an ever-increasing number of bots. By 2024, </span><a href="https://www.imperva.com/blog/bad-bot-report-2026-bots-agentic-age/">automated traffic had surpassed human traffic</a><span>, and just last month, Cloudflare announced that </span><a href="https://www.forbes.com/sites/josipamajic/2026/06/04/bots-now-outnumber-humans-online-and-the-internet-was-never-built-for-this/">bots had reached 57.5% of web page requests</a><span>.</span></p><p>The Numbers felt this shift in two distinct waves. The first started around 2024:</p><blockquote><p>We saw a big increase in crawls as AI training joined the search engine crawlers. The AI crawlers are generally less well-behaved than the search engines, which increased the management tasks for us to keep the site running smoothly.</p></blockquote><p>And the second wave was stronger and more damaging:</p><blockquote><p>Around December 2025, we saw another big spike in traffic which I attribute to agentic AI: a combination of AI agents that scrape sites in response to prompts, and people being able to write agents that scrape sites. </p></blockquote><p>Like every data-rich site, by early 2026 The Numbers was being hammered hard by AI bots scraping its pages over and over at an industrial scale.
