# Title:LLM-powered reasoning in agent-based modeling
Authors:[Sifat Afroj Moon](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Moon,+S+A), [Dakotah Maguire](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Maguire,+D), [Adam Spannaus](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Spannaus,+A), [Joe Tuccillo](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Tuccillo,+J), [Maksudul Alam](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Alam,+M), [Sudip K. Seal](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Seal,+S+K), [John Gounley](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Gounley,+J), [Heidi Hanson](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Hanson,+H) View a PDF of the paper titled LLM-powered reasoning in agent-based modeling, by Sifat Afroj Moon and 7 other authors
[View PDF](/pdf/2607.06757)
[HTML (experimental)](https://arxiv.org/html/2607.06757v1)
>
Abstract:Agent-based modeling (ABM) has the capability to model millions of individuals and their interactions, which is useful for policy making. However, ABMs have traditionally relied on static prior, which prevents the models from adapting to real-time changes. Our research provides a novel approach to addressing this information gap. Large language models (LLMs) offer new opportunities to predict human decision-making. Here, we introduce a scalable Hybrid Agent-based and Language-driven Epidemic (HALE) modeling framework that leverages LLMs to predict human decision-making in an ABM simulation. As a proof-of-concept, we use HALE to simulate COVID-19 and its effects in Salt Lake County, UT.
