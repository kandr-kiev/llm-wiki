# Title:AI-integrated models for assessing agricultural resilience
Authors:[Joshua R. Waite](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Waite,+J+R), [Dana Golden](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Golden,+D), [Brett Indelicato](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Indelicato,+B), [Kevin Camp](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Camp,+K), [Mojdeh Saadati](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Saadati,+M), [Shannon Regan](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Regan,+S), [Patrick Schnable](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Schnable,+P), [Baskar Ganapathysubramanian](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Ganapathysubramanian,+B), [Carlos Messina](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Messina,+C), [Suzanne Thornsbury](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Thornsbury,+S), [Soumik Sarkar](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Sarkar,+S) View a PDF of the paper titled AI-integrated models for assessing agricultural resilience, by Joshua R. Waite and 10 other authors
[View PDF](/pdf/2607.07759)
>
Abstract:Agricultural supply chains are vulnerable to disruptions through linked biophysical and economic systems. We develop an AI-powered tool that integrates economic models (GTAP) with biophysical models (APSIM) to analyze supply chain shocks, enabling policymakers and market participants to assess cross-disciplinary impacts through queries and responses written in natural language.
