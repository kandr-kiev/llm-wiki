# Title:DialogueVPR: Towards Conversational Visual Place Recognition
Authors:[Yukun Song](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Song,+Y), [Changwei Wang](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Wang,+C), [Xingtian Pei](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Pei,+X), [Shibiao Xu](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Xu,+S), [Wenhao Xu](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Xu,+W), [Shunpeng Chen](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Chen,+S), [Yu Zhang](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Zhang,+Y), [Ke Zhang](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Zhang,+K), [Rongtao Xu](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Xu,+R), [Xuxiang Feng](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Feng,+X), [Pengyang Wang](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Wang,+P) View a PDF of the paper titled DialogueVPR: Towards Conversational Visual Place Recognition, by Yukun Song and 10 other authors
[View PDF](/pdf/2607.14115)
[HTML (experimental)](https://arxiv.org/html/2607.14115v1)
>
Abstract:Inspired by how humans communicate spatial information, language-guided geo-localization has gained significant traction for its intuitive and practical value. Despite this progress, most methods still rely on a static, one-shot retrieval paradigm, which fails to handle the ambiguity and incompleteness inherent in real-world natural language descriptions. We propose a paradigm shift to reasoning retrieval and introduce Dialogue Place Recognition (DlgPR), which casts localization as an interactive, dialogue-driven reasoning process. To support this new task, we present DlgQuest-Cities, the first large-scale dialogue-based benchmark for place recognition, and a unified reasoning framework that couples a cross-modal multi-level retriever with an intelligent questioner, DQ-pilot. DQ-pilot is trained in a curriculum: supervised fine-tuning on a curated DQ-cities-20k subset followed by reinforcement refinement on a harder DQ-cities-10k split via GRPO. Two task-aligned metrics guide learning: a Discriminative Difficulty Index (DDI) for curriculum sampling and a Positional Retrieval Gain (PRG) reward that directly measures retrieval improvement induced by a question. Experiments show this reasoning-based approach significantly outperforms baselines. The code and model are available at [this https URL](https://github.com/Graysonggg/DlgPR).
