# 
How to Read Scripts While Recording Camera Video in the Browser
[#tutorial](/t/tutorial)
[#productivity](/t/productivity)
[#webdev](/t/webdev)
[#beginners](/t/beginners)
Recording a scripted video sounds simple until you try to do three things at once: remember the message, look at the camera, and keep your delivery natural.
Most people solve one problem and create another. A full script keeps the message clear, but it can make your eyes scan the screen. Bullet points sound more natural, but you may forget important details. A teleprompter helps, but only if the script and reading pace are set up for spoken delivery instead of written prose.
This guide is a practical workflow for recording product demos, tutorials, course lessons, webinars, and short creator videos in the browser.

Start With Spoken Writing

A recording script should be easier to speak than to read silently. Use contractions. Break long sentences. Replace formal transitions with phrases you would actually say.
Instead of:
>
In this tutorial, we will be demonstrating the complete process by which users can configure the recording workflow.
Try:
>
In this tutorial, I will show you how to set up the recording workflow step by step.
The second version is not shorter just for SEO or readability. It is easier to say without sounding like you are reciting.
## 
Use Short Paragraph Blocks
Long teleprompter blocks make your eyes travel too far. Even if the words are good, the delivery can look distracted.
A better pattern is to split the script into short blocks:
```
Open with the problem.
Show the first action.
Explain why it matters.
Pause before the next step.
```
Enter fullscreen mode
Exit fullscreen mode
This makes the script feel closer to a set of spoken beats. You still know what to say, but the layout gives you places to breathe.
## 
Pick WPM By Video Type
Words per minute matters more than most people expect. If the speed is too fast, you sound rushed. If it is too slow, the recording can feel flat.
Good starting ranges:
Video type
Starting WPM
Calm tutorial or course lesson
105-125
Product demo
120-145
Short social video
135-165
Webinar intro
110-135
Formal presentation
95-120
These are only starting points. A technical tutorial usually needs more space between sentences than a short product teaser.
## 
Keep The Camera Near The Script
The closer your script is to the camera, the better your eye contact will feel.
If you are using a browser teleprompter, put the recording window and script where your eyes do not need to move across the whole screen. Increase the font size before you increase the scrolling speed. Big readable text usually looks more natural than tiny text moving quickly.
## 
Rehearse Once, Then Record
Too little rehearsal creates uncertainty. Too much rehearsal can make the delivery sound memorized.
A useful middle ground is one quick rehearsal where you check:
- Does any sentence feel hard to say?
- Are the transitions clear?
- Are there obvious pause points?
- Is the WPM comfortable?
- Does the first 10 seconds sound natural?
Then record. If the first take is awkward, adjust the script instead of simply forcing another take.
## 
Record A Short Test Clip
Before recording the full video, record 10-15 seconds and check:
- eye contact
- reading speed
- audio level
- camera framing
- whether your eyes visibly scan
- whether the script sounds like speech
This tiny test saves more time than trying to fix an entire recording afterward.

- Write the script in short spoken blocks.
- Paste it into a browser teleprompter.
- Set WPM based on the video type.
- Increase font size until eye movement is minimal.
