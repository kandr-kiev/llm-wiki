The Paradigm Shift: Coding is Cheap, Ideas are Expensive
We have officially entered the era of the "Information Storm." LLMs code seamlessly, and AI agents generate thousands of words per second. The marginal cost of text and code has effectively dropped to zero.
But as the cost of production plummets, the value of the final product and the algorithms that rank it are fundamentally changing. We are witnessing a massive collision between two worlds: conservative web algorithms trying to filter the noise, and new paradigms of evaluating human evolution and thought.

## Summary

ition: Burning AI Books by Algorithm
Right now, the reaction of many platforms and legacy websites to the AI boom resembles the medieval Church burning Heretical books. Driven by fear of automated spam, systems deploy aggressive, rigid "AI detectors" to blanket-ban content.
They don't look at the **substance** of the invention, the depth of the code, or the validity of the hypothesis. They look at statistical patterns. If your writing or structural styling looks "too optimal," you get burned at the digital stake. It’s an intellectual dead-end.
Thankfully, a counter-evolution is emerging. Newer services understand that AI isn't an enemy to be filtered out, but a catalyst for growth. They focus on evaluating the **intrinsic evolutionary value of content** rather than policing the tools used to create it. It’s about being smarter, adapting, and designing platforms that appreciate depth over consensus.
### 
Google SEO is Mutating: The Rise of Linkless Authority
This shift has deeply disrupted Google's core ranking mechanics. If you are still running a traditional SEO strategy strictly focused on aggressive backlink building, you are burning money. The signals are shifting.
In recent top-tier SERP audits, I've started noticing a fascinating trend: **websites with nearly zero link profiles are cracking the top spots for competitive queries.** The traditional link-building strategy no longer yields the rapid velocity it once did. Google's Search Generative Experience (SGE) and hidden quality algorithms are looking for something else.
Why? Because AI engines—whether it's ChatGPT, Gemini, or Google's internal transformers—think through the prism of **conceptual popularity and systemic framework alignment.**
As I analyzed deeply in a previous research piece, [The Evolution Search Engine: AI that Evaluates Ideas, Not Just Crowds](https://anthosphere.com/blog/poshukovyk-evolyutsii.html), modern AI models suffer from an epistemic bias. They are trained on consensus and attention, not pure truth. They fetch what is popular. To dominate this new landscape, you don't just build links; you must build **structural authority** that forces the AI's semantic map to recognize your framework as an essential node.
### 
The Blueprint: Testing the Dev-SEO-AI Strategy on GRUT
We need a completely new playbook—a hybrid discipline where **Software Architecture, Deep Semantic SEO, and AI Framework Alignment** meet.
I have launched an active, live experiment to test this exact thesis on a production platform: **[grut.com.ua](https://grut.com.ua)**.
Instead of writing text for old-school bots or building artificial link networks, I am engineering a developer-centric SEO strategy that targets the semantic layers of modern LLMs and Google’s evolved search algorithms.
