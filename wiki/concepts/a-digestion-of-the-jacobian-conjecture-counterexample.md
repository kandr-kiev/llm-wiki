# A digestion of the Jacobian conjecture counterexample
21 July, 2026 in [math.AG](https://terrytao.wordpress.com/category/mathematics/mathag/) | Tags: [Jacobian conjecture](https://terrytao.wordpress.com/tag/jacobian-conjecture/), [polynomials](https://terrytao.wordpress.com/tag/polynomials/) | by [Terence Tao](https://terrytao.wordpress.com/author/teorth/) 
The notorious [Jacobian conjecture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacobian_conjecture) can be formulated concretely over the complex numbers as follows.
>**Conjecture 1 (Jacobian Conjecture)** Let ![{F:{\bf C}^n \rightarrow {\bf C}^n}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%7BF%3A%7B%5Cbf+C%7D%5En+%5Crightarrow+%7B%5Cbf+C%7D%5En%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=000000&s=0&c=20201002) be a polynomial map in ![{n}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%7Bn%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=000000&s=0&c=20201002) complex variables, whose Jacobian ![{\mathrm{det} DF}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%7B%5Cmathrm%7Bdet%7D+DF%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=000000&s=0&c=20201002) is a non-zero constant. Then ![{F}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%7BF%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=000000&s=0&c=20201002) is invertible (with polynomial inverse). 
The condition that the Jacobian ![{\mathrm{det} DF}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%7B%5Cmathrm%7Bdet%7D+DF%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=000000&s=0&c=20201002) is non-zero is equivalent to ![{F}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%7BF%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=000000&s=0&c=20201002) being locally invertible. (The implication of local invertibility from non-vanishing Jacobian follows from the inverse function theorem; the converse implication can be derived from the [Weierstrass preparation theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weierstrass_preparation_theorem), but is omitted here.) Also, from the fundamental theorem of algebra, once the Jacobian polynomial ![{\mathrm{det} DF}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%7B%5Cmathrm%7Bdet%7D+DF%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=000000&s=0&c=20201002) is non-zero, it must be constant. So the Jacobian conjecture can be viewed as an assertion that local invertibility implies global invertibility. The complex numbers can be easily replaced with other fields of characteristic zero by the [Lefschetz principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algebraic_geometry_and_analytic_geometry#The_Lefschetz_principle), but I prefer to work in the concrete setting of the complex numbers.
Recently, it was [recently shown](https://www.newscientist.com/article/2580374-ais-solution-to-87-year-old-riddle-takes-mathematicians-by-surprise/) (using the Fable AI) that the conjecture is false in three dimensions (and thus in higher dimensions as well):
>**Theorem 2 (Counterexample to conjecture)** There exists a polynomial ![{F : {\bf C}^3 \rightarrow {\bf C}^3}](https://s0.wp.com/latex.php?latex=%7BF+%3A+%7B%5Cbf+C%7D%5E3+%5Crightarrow+%7B%5Cbf+C%7D%5E3%7D&bg=ffffff&fg=000000&s=0&c=20201002) which has non-zero constant Jacobian, but is not invertible. 
The conjecture remains open in two dimensions, and is easy to establish in one dimension.
