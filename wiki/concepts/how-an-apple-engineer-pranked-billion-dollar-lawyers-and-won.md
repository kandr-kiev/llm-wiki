# How an Apple Engineer Pranked Billion-Dollar Lawyers (and Won)
![Beau Carnes](https://cdn.hashnode.com/res/hashnode/image/upload/v1713211849730/O5mmKs5h0.jpg)
[

](/news/author/beaucarnes/)
![How an Apple Engineer Pranked Billion-Dollar Lawyers (and Won)](https://cdn.hashnode.com/uploads/covers/5f68e7df6dfc523d0a894e7c/edbee5f7-49b7-483b-9a4c-041143d823c2.png)
What happens when an engineer gets fed up with corporate lawyers breathing down their neck? Sometimes, you get an inside joke that quietly ships to millions of machines and survives for over three decades.
Back in the late 1980s, amidst a fierce multi-million dollar trademark battle, Apple's legal team began heavily scrutinizing every technical asset, line of code, and audio file for any accidental musical references. Pushed to the limit by the endless bureaucratic interference, one engineer decided to orchestrate a brilliant, passive-aggressive act of defiance.
Through a clever piece of misdirection on written paperwork, a deeply sarcastic inside joke slipped right past legal review and became a permanent part of the operating system. Over thirty years later, that hidden act of rebellion is still embedded in unexpected places across modern tech history, including the very code styling Apple's own legal disclaimers.
Uncover the full story of how a rogue sound designer outsmarted the legal department on [the freeCodeCamp.org YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PVAstrvWPkQ) (7-minute watch).
